# The Ascension of Christ

[[Austin Sparks]] | Source: *The Ascension of Christ*

---

## Underappreciated and Misunderstood

Sparks' opening observation: the Ascension receives far less attention in Christian tradition than Christmas, Good Friday, or Pentecost, yet it carries immense governing significance. Understanding what the Ascension accomplishes changes what every action in the Christian life is *for*.

---

## The Wherefore — Why He Ascended

Eph. 4 is the governing text. Christ descended "into the lower parts of the earth" — experiencing the depths of human depravity and sin — and then ascended "far above all heavens." The purpose is captured in one word: *"wherefore"* (Eph. 4:10). He ascended so "that He might fill all things."

This phrase — filling all things — is not abstract. It means that Christ intends to take full possession of every domain, relationship, and situation that exists. The Ascension is not a departure; it is an expansion of Christ's operative presence into everything.

---

## The Distribution of Gifts

Upon ascending, Christ did not simply claim victory and rest. He actively distributed gifts to His Church: "some apostles; and some, prophets; and some, evangelists; and some, pastors and teachers" (Eph. 4:11). These gifts are not decorative — they are instruments for advancing His programme of filling all things. Every gift-ministry exists for one purpose: bringing Christ more fully into situations.

The test of all ministry, therefore, is singular: is it providing ground for Christ to get a fuller possession of this person, this family, this gathering, this situation?

---

## The Practical Governing Principle

The "wherefore" principle extends beyond formal ministry to all of life:
- Home
- Business and occupation
- Personal relationships
- Appearance and conduct

Each domain becomes a question: does this provide ground for Christ to fill this area more completely? The Ascension is not a past event to celebrate once a year; it is the governing rationale for every present action. Christ is actively working at God's right hand, continuously giving and appointing to accomplish His filling purpose (Ps. 68; Heb. 12).
