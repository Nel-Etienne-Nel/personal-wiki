# Ministry, Vision, and Calling

[[Austin Sparks]] | Sources: *Accredited Ministry*, *The All-governing Vision in the Life of the Child of God*, *An Apostle's Supreme Ambition*

---

## What Accredits Ministry — Suffering and Resurrection

True spiritual ministry receives God's accreditation through suffering, not through claiming authority or presenting credentials. The hallmark of God-accredited ministry is resurrection: the vessel is broken, and then God demonstrates that the work survives and cannot be destroyed. "Resurrection becomes the hallmark of that which is accredited of God."

The enemy's strategy against ministers: creating a sense of unworthiness, associating the ministry with compromised influences, using patronisation to weaken spiritual clarity. God's counter-strategy: He keeps the vessel in weakness, dependence upon Himself, so "all is kept spiritual, and all is of the Lord alone."

Two contemporary tragedies Sparks identifies:
1. Many believers lack adequate spiritual knowledge — they remain immature
2. Many possess knowledge but lack living power — they are spiritually dead

Standing for what is purely spiritual requires willingness to suffer loss. But such suffering produces ministry that genuinely counts. Key references: 2 Cor. 1:8–9; 4:1, 10–11; 1 Cor. 2:3–5.

## The All-governing Vision — God's Dwelling with His People

The foundational idea underneath all of Scripture: God's intention to dwell with people. Rev. 21:3: "The tabernacle of God is with men, and He shall dwell with them." This is not a destination added at the end of history; it is the purpose governing all divine labour, sacrifice, and conflict from the beginning. All ministry either serves this purpose or it is directionless.

Three essential elements of God's dwelling-place:
- **God's Presence** — immediate and intimate fellowship (Emmanuel)
- **God's Glory** — the manifestation of divine perfection when evil is eliminated
- **God's Worship** — the expression of reverence and service

God's house is not a building; it is a people united with Christ as Head (Heb. 3:6). Building this house involves two inseparable processes:
1. Numerical growth — gathering individuals through evangelism
2. Spiritual development — building up believers in faith and maturity

Both require equal emphasis. Local expressions of the church serve a crucial purpose: demonstrating Christ's greatness in relationships, training believers for ultimate eternal function, and providing a concrete site for experiencing God's presence and glory. The present age is gathering material for God's eternal sanctuary.

## An Apostle's Supreme Ambition — Philippians 3:10

Paul's declaration: "That I may know Him, and the power of His resurrection, and the fellowship of His sufferings, becoming conformed unto His death" — Sparks reads this as a programme, not a statement.

Four movements:

| Element | Nature |
|---|---|
| "That I may know Him" | Experiential, personal knowledge — not information *about* Christ but transformation *by* Christ through the Spirit |
| "The power of His resurrection" | Supernatural capability operative in the present for endurance, victory, and ministry — "the greatest miracle in history" made accessible |
| "The fellowship of His sufferings" | Not Christ's atoning sufferings, but the persecution, rejection, and sacrifice that come from identification with Him; the basis of genuine spiritual usefulness |
| "Becoming conformed unto His death" | Progressive crucifixion of the old self — the ongoing condition that enables ever-deeper knowledge and ever-greater resurrection power |

The progression is important: knowledge of Christ requires resurrection power, which requires the fellowship of His sufferings, which requires conformity to His death. You cannot take the top of the list while bypassing the bottom.

"They that have suffered most have most to give."

## How These Three Connect

True ministry is accredited by God through suffering → it serves the single governing vision of God dwelling with His people → the personal disposition required for it is Paul's own ambition: knowing Christ experientially through the cross-resurrection pattern. All three articles define what authentic, God-given ministry looks like from three angles: credential, purpose, and personal disposition.
