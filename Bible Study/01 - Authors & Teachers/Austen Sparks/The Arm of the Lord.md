# The Arm of the Lord

[[Austin Sparks]] | Source: *The Arm of the Lord*

---

## The Cross as God's Exclusive Remedy

The Cross of Christ is God's exclusive remedy for spiritual maladies in individual believers, churches, and the world. The revealing of God's power depends upon believers accepting the Cross's work in their lives — not selectively or partially, but comprehensively.

Isa. 52:13–53:11: the Cross deals with sin, transgressions, iniquities, and spiritual infirmities in one movement. There is no supplementary remedy. Sparks lists the objections believers raise: the Cross is unpopular, difficult, repetitive as teaching, and it contradicts natural preferences. None of these change the diagnosis or the prescription.

---

## The Cross as Positive Force

The most important correction Sparks makes: the Cross functions *constructively*, not destructively. This is counter-intuitive. The Cross does not annihilate — it *clears ground for building*. The Arm of the Lord operates toward two objectives:
1. Clearing away impediments to God's work
2. Exalting and vindicating Jesus Christ

The "No" of the Cross (God's negation of the flesh and self-assertion) is the necessary precondition for the "Yes" — 2 Cor. 1:20. God uses the Cross to strip believers of pride and self-credit specifically so that any spiritual growth glorifies Christ alone, not human leaders or human effort.

---

## The Historical Pattern and the Present Decline

Early Christians' willingness to preach "Christ crucified" despite persecution and social rejection was what revealed God's power. Modern spiritual decline in the Church stems from attempts to soften or avoid the Cross's offence — to make it more palatable, more culturally accessible, less demanding. The result is not a friendlier gospel but a powerless one.

The Cross must apply in personal relationships, circumstances, and self-perception. When believers are genuinely willing for the Cross to work there — not just as doctrine but as lived experience — the Church is built and Christ is glorified.

Key references: Isa. 52:13; 53:10–11; Phil. 2:8–9; Rom. 6–8; 2 Cor. 1:20; Isa. 65:16; Gal. 6:14.
