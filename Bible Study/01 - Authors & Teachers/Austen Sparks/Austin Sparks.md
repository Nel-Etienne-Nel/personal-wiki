Austin Sparks - All things Christ

**The Core Problem Sparks Identifies**

Sparks is concerned about something subtle but devastating: people take the truths of the Bible and turn them into _systems_ or _things_ that get detached from the living Person of Jesus Christ. The Epistles, for example, were meant to reveal Christ — but Christianity has tended to extract doctrines from them and build "isms" around those doctrines. You get sanctificationists, adventists, Reformed camps, charismatic camps, etc. Each group has taken a _truth_ and made it an _object of focus_ in its own right, separate from Christ Himself.

**The Examples He Uses**

Sparks works through several major biblical themes to make his point:

_The Kingdom of God_ — He says it is not a future political arrangement or a system to be analyzed. The Kingdom of God _is_ Christ. If you are in Christ, you are in the Kingdom. The Kingdom, when fully manifested at the end, will simply be the full expression of Christ in all things.

_Sanctification_ — He points to 1 Corinthians 1:30: Christ "is made unto us sanctification." So sanctification is not a doctrine or an experience you chase after and arrive at. It is knowing Christ more deeply. Sparks makes the fascinating observation that the teaching of sanctification _as a thing_ has actually caused more harm and confusion among Christians than almost any other doctrine — precisely because it was made into a destination rather than kept as an expression of union with Christ.

_The Second Coming_ — This is where he gets most pointed. He's not dismissing prophecy, but he's challenging people who are fascinated with prophetic charts — revival of the Roman Empire, Russia, Palestine, etc. — while having no deep inward walk with God. He tells the story of following a prophecy teacher and watching notebooks snap shut the moment he shifted from external signs to the inward, purifying effect of that hope (1 John 3:3). His point: the best preparation for Christ's return is _knowing Christ now_, because His coming will simply be the outward manifestation of what has been happening inwardly in His people.

**The Central Solution: The School of Christ**

Sparks says the Holy Spirit's job is not to teach us _things about_ Christ, but to teach us _Christ Himself_ — experientially, personally, progressively. The moment a truth becomes a "thing," a system, a camp — it becomes earthly. But when every truth (kingdom, sanctification, the coming, the church, everything) is held as an expression of the living Person, then the Holy Spirit can work freely.

**Why This Matters for Your Study**

This connects powerfully to what you've been discovering — how the covenants, the Law, the sacrificial system, all of it points to Christ rather than existing as ends in themselves. The Mosaic Law wasn't the destination; Christ was. Sparks is essentially applying that same principle to _all_ Christian teaching: every doctrine is meant to be a window through which you see Christ more clearly, not a room where you set up camp.

The warning is worth sitting with — it's possible to be deeply knowledgeable about prophecy, sanctification, ecclesiology, even covenant theology, and yet subtly drift from Christ Himself as the center. The test Sparks gives is simple: is knowing this drawing me closer to the Person of Jesus, or is it drawing me closer to a system?
