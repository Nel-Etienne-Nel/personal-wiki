# The Anointing

[[Austin Sparks]] | Sources: *The Anointing*, *The Anointing: Light Within Our Dwellings*, *Anointed for Battle*

---

## The Active Dimension of the Spirit

Sparks distinguishes between possessing the Spirit and the anointing. Believers may have the Spirit dwelling within them latently; the anointing is the *active* dimension — characterized by purposeful, energetic action. The Holy Spirit operates with divine purpose but waits for believers to move first. Passivity allows the Spirit to remain dormant despite indwelling presence.

Biblical pattern: priests were anointed to serve, kings to rule, prophets to proclaim. Jesus received the anointing for active ministry — "preaching Good Tidings" and healing the oppressed (Acts 10:38; Luke 4:18). The basis of the anointing is deep inward separation: separation from Satan's kingdom, particularly pride. Humility and meekness are the prerequisites; Jesus exemplified this through self-emptying and dependence on the Father.

Practically: avoid quenching the Spirit through either frivolity/talkativeness or artificial heaviness. Maintain sensitivity to divine guidance. Believers must move decisively; the Spirit responds with empowerment.

Key references: 2 Cor. 1:21–22; 1 John 2:20, 27; Ezek. 28:14; 1 Thess. 5:19

## Light Within Our Dwellings — The Anointing as Internal Authority

Those truly united with Christ possess "light in their dwelling" through the anointing. 1 John 2:27: believers "need not that any one teach you, but...His anointing teacheth you concerning all things."

Sparks is not anti-teaching; he is against *dependence* that bypasses the Spirit's witness. Excessive reliance on human authorities, even godly ones, creates spiritual bondage. Believers build weak foundations when their convictions rest on others' conclusions rather than the Spirit's confirmation.

Paul's example in Galatians 1:15–17 is instructive: after receiving revelation of the Gentile mission, he did not submit it to Jerusalem's apostles for validation but tested it against the Spirit's confirmation. This independence from human authority (not arrogance — Spirit-dependence, not self-dependence) enabled apostolic ministry.

The principle Sparks calls *resurrection ground*: spiritual knowledge flourishes only when believers operate from Spirit-governance over natural reasoning and human hierarchy. Human authorities should inform but never determine spiritual conviction.

Note: Paul continued seeking deeper knowledge even late in his ministry (Phil. 3:8). No teacher exhausts Christ's meaning.

## Anointed for Battle — The Primary Purpose of the Anointing

The Holy Spirit's primary function is establishing Christ's sovereignty over spiritual rebellion through His Church. The anointing is not for personal comfort; it is for warfare. Sparks: "The primary purpose of the anointing with the Spirit is the establishment of the sovereignty of Christ through His Church."

Just as physical health depends on the body's internal warfare against pathogens, spiritual vitality requires constant conflict. The Church cannot be vigorous without warfare. Difficulty and trial prove and strengthen authentic faith. Not all conflicts are healthy — some represent spiritual disorder (pathological difficulties) that produce weakness rather than strength. The discernment between necessary spiritual battle and destructive internal pathology matters.

Against spiritual passivity: seeking escape from conflict, settling into comfort, taking "spiritual holidays" — all of these represent disengagement that causes spiritual atrophy. The enemy specifically tempts believers toward this.

Scripture anchor: Matthew's account of Christ's baptism (anointing) immediately followed by wilderness temptation. The anointing initiated conflict, not comfort.

## How These Three Connect

The anointing is active, not passive → its source is the indwelling Spirit discerned from within → its purpose is war, not blessing. Sparks is building a case against a comfort-centred, externally-dependent spirituality and for an inwardly-governed, warfare-ready life in the Spirit.
