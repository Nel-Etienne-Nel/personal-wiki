# The Altar — Cross as Governing Principle

[[Austin Sparks]] | Sources: *The Altar (The Cross) Governs Everything*, *The Altar and the Name*, *The Altar of Incense*

---

## The Cross as Center

In Ezekiel's temple vision the brass altar stood at the geometric centre — all lines converged on it. Sparks reads this architecturally: when the Cross occupies its proper place with its full dimensions, everything else in the church, in ministry, and in the life of a believer "sees to itself." Problems of order, carnality, and false spirituality do not need to be fixed directly; they dissolve when the Cross is genuinely central.

The altar's two realities: the natural man is brought to ashes (consumed in judgment); the righteous man survives indestructible. Christ embodies both — made sin and consumed, yet sinless and indestructible. Paul demonstrates the Cross's varied applications across his letters:

| Epistle | What the Cross judges |
|---|---|
| Romans | The entire race in Adam |
| 1 Corinthians | Carnality within the church |
| Galatians | Legalism and bondage |
| Ephesians | The heavenly church's foundation |
| Colossians | False spirituality |

Galatians 6:14 — the crucified believer remains protected from "the world." Satan's preferred strategy is not frontal assault but infiltration with worldly values; the Cross alone makes that impossible.

The Cross is not a doctrine to be studied. Sparks is insistent: "it is power. The Cross is an experience...a crisis...a revolution...an earthquake."

---

## The Altar and the Name — Internal Victory First

The altar and the divine Name are inseparably linked throughout Scripture. Gideon's pivotal act before any external victory was building an altar named *Jehovah-Shalom* — the Lord-Peace. Inner conquest precedes outer triumph. The Name of God requires a vessel emptied of self-glory:

- A fundamental reckoning: counting all loss for Christ (Phil. 2:9–10)
- Complete emptying of personal glory
- Willing obedience unto death

Each generation must freshly receive and carry forward spiritual values — they cannot be inherited from the previous one. The enemy gains no foothold within if the Name is genuinely honoured at the altar.

## The Altar of Incense — Prayer as the Underlayer of Everything

The altar of incense is placed last in the tabernacle instructions, not as an afterthought but as a statement of weight: "everything is carried on and made effectual through prayer." Divorced from prayer, all the other provisions remain largely ineffectual.

The daily maintenance of the incense altar (Ex. 30:7–8):
- **Morning** — trimming the lamps: removing fleshly manifestations through prayer
- **Evening** — lighting the lamps: maintaining spiritual testimony against darkness through intercession

The horns of the altar represent strength — and this strength is not separate from prayer but *is* prayer. The gold crown atop the altar reflects Christ's victorious intercession (Isa. 53; Heb. 2:9), inviting believers to participate in that ministry.

## How These Three Connect

The Altar (Cross) governs everything → The Name is vindicated through the Cross → Prayer flows from the Cross and makes everything effectual. The three form a single architecture: the Cross as the centre, the Cross as the basis of identity and Name, the Cross as the ground from which all intercession rises.

→ [[The Anointing]] — Sparks' anointing articles show the same logic from the Spirit's side: the Spirit operates on the basis of what the Cross established.
→ [[The Arm of the Lord]] — the Cross as God's exclusive remedy for spiritual decline.
→ [[Austin Sparks]] — this is the Cross-face of his core thesis that Christ Himself (crucified) is the centre, not a doctrine of the Cross.
