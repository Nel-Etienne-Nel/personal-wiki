# An Appeal

[[Austin Sparks]] | Source: *An Appeal*

---

## The Problem — Earthly Division

The Church exhibits "earthly" behaviour: divisions, rivalries, natural preferences, sectarian allegiances. Paul's rebuke in 1 Cor. 3:4 — "Are ye not men?" — suggests that believers are acting according to worldly standards rather than spiritual ones. The fragmentation is not a doctrinal accident; it is the result of operating on the wrong ground.

---

## Two Foundations for Unity

**First appeal: Heavenly ground**

Sparks contrasts 1 Corinthians (earthly perspective, the problem) with Ephesians (heavenly reality, the solution). Ephesians demonstrates "the one body," "the unity of the Spirit," and believers "seated together with Him in the heavenlies" (Eph. 2:6). This transcendent position provides ascendency over earthly limitations. From heaven's vantage point, the divisions that seem insurmountable at ground level lose their defining power.

**Second appeal: The ground of the Cross**

The Cross operates on two levels here. First, it addresses sins and condemnation — already understood. Second (and this is Sparks' emphasis), it eradicates the *natural ground* that produces jealousy, rivalry, and contention in the first place. When believers genuinely appropriate the Cross's transformative power, the soil in which division grows is removed. The result is not forced unity but an organic, loving concern for all God's people that cuts across sectarian boundaries.

The two grounds belong together: heavenly position provides the perspective; the Cross does the excavation.

Key references: 1 Cor. 3:4; 2:2; Ephesians (throughout).
