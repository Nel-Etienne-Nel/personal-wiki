# All Things in Christ

[[Austin Sparks]] | Sources: *All Things in Christ*, *Attaining Unto the First Three*, *Attaining to God's Full Thought*, *The Attesting of Christ*

---

## The Core Problem — Doctrines Detached from Christ

This is the heart of Sparks' entire body of work (see [[Austin Sparks]] for the full thesis). Christian teaching has fragmented into isolated doctrines and systems — sanctificationists, prophecy enthusiasts, Reformed camps — each taking a truth and making it a *thing* in its own right, detached from the living Person. The result: every "ism" becomes an earthly system.

The test he gives: is knowing this drawing me closer to the *Person* of Jesus, or is it drawing me closer to a system? Sanctification is not a destination or an experience to chase; Christ *is* made unto us sanctification (1 Cor. 1:30). The kingdom of God is not a future political arrangement to be charted; it is Christ. Prophecy is not about geopolitical signs but about the purifying inward work of hope — the best preparation for Christ's return is *knowing Christ now*, because His coming will simply be the outward manifestation of what has been happening inwardly.

The Holy Spirit's proper work is teaching believers Christ experientially, not transmitting doctrinal systems.

## Attaining Unto the First Three — Pursuing Maximum Spiritual Greatness

From David's mighty men (2 Sam. 23), Sparks identifies four marks of those who reach the highest place of usefulness to God:

1. **Perceiving God's full thought** when others cannot see it — recognising that the present order does not align with God's ultimate purpose, and committing to nothing less than His complete design
2. **Personal responsibility without external support** — "there may be no one else here for it, but because I have seen it I refuse to abandon it." No waiting for organisational backing.
3. **Spiritual stamina** — finishing what one starts; Eleazar's "hand clave unto the sword" (2 Sam. 23:10) demonstrates commitment through weariness
4. **Establishing the absolute lordship of Christ** — not a personal achievement but a purpose; the first three mighty men stand *for* something, not merely *against* something

The direct challenge: will believers settle for mediocrity or pursue the costly path of full alignment with God's purpose? The church desperately needs those who will attain to the first three.

## Attaining to God's Full Thought — The Remnant Principle

When the general body of believers fails God, He works through a believing remnant. This is not elitism; it is God's consistent pattern through Scripture.

The Babylonian captivity as the model: most Israelites settled contentedly in Babylon. A remnant maintained dissatisfaction, longing for God's fuller expression. Daniel's intercession kept this alive. Then God sovereignly stirred Cyrus — "I have girded thee, though thou hast not known me" (Isa. 45:5) — to accomplish purposes Cyrus didn't understand.

Two essential components:
- **Ezra** — divine sovereignty: God acting to accomplish His purposes
- **Nehemiah** — human faithfulness: believers responding with commitment and wisdom

Both are required. Those called to God's fuller purposes experience difficult, tortuous pathways; repeated cycles of departure and return to deeper commitment; seemingly contradictory circumstances. Paul's later epistles (Ephesians, Philippians, Colossians) represent God's sovereign provision against spiritual declension — guidance for a dark day.

The ultimate question: either God is sovereign and faithful, or He abandons devoted believers to chaos. There is no middle ground.

## The Attesting of Christ — Transformed Lives as the Primary Evidence

God's predominant objective in all His workings is *the attesting of Christ* — demonstrating Christ's identity and authority so that people believe and experience eternal life. Dark, mysterious circumstances in a believer's life only find meaning when related to this purpose.

The method is personal transformation, not doctrinal argument. The man born blind (John 9) progresses from "the man called Jesus" to "God's Son" — stages of increasing vision. Believers become living witnesses through the Holy Spirit's power working in and through them. Deepening spiritual sight inevitably brings separation from institutional religion and worldly society, yet results in being gathered into Christ's fold.

The cost is real. The purpose is singular.
