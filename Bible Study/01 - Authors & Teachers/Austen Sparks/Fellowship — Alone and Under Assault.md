# Fellowship — Alone and Under Assault

[[Austin Sparks]] | Sources: *Alone? — Not Alone!*, *The Assault on Fellowship*

---

## Not Alone — The Lie Satan Plants in Exhaustion

God's servants who experience loneliness in spiritual leadership should recognise this as a common experience with a common source, not a sign of abandonment or uniqueness. Satan targets vulnerable moments — when spiritual leaders are physically or emotionally exhausted — to plant one specific lie: that they stand alone in faithfulness to God.

Elijah believed he was "the only one left" faithful to God. The Lord revealed there were actually seven thousand in Israel who had not compromised (1 Kings 19:18). Elijah's perception did not match reality. His experience was real; his conclusion was false.

Three consoling truths Sparks draws out:
1. God's love accounts for human weakness *before* calling anyone to service — weakness is not disqualifying
2. The Lord requires only a sincere heart directed toward Him — sincerity, not perfection
3. Believers are never truly isolated; they participate in "the communion of the Holy Spirit" with the broader body of Christ (1 Cor. 12:12)

Some loneliness accompanies genuine spiritual advancement and value in God's work. This is not a contradiction: the loneliness is real, but the isolation is not. Moses, David, and Peter all experienced failure and seasons of apparent abandonment without losing their spiritual significance and purpose.

## The Assault on Fellowship — Division as Spiritual Warfare

Divisions among believers are not fundamentally personality conflicts — they are Satanic attacks on the Lord's testimony. What appears as natural incompatibility often masks spiritual warfare. The enemy specifically targets fellowship between believers, especially those with responsibility for God's testimony.

The practical implication is significant: the solution to division is not adjusting personalities to work together naturally. It is recognising the spiritual dimension and maintaining unity as an *act of resistance* against Satan's assault. This shift in perspective creates "a dynamic for fellowship" that personality adjustment never could.

The broader principle Sparks adds: the heavenly calling is always positive, never negative, neutral, or passive. Even mundane daily tasks serve God's purposes. God uses ordinary, uninteresting situations — not only dramatic trials — to develop faith, patience, and endurance. These are the elements that constitute what Sparks calls "the throne's power." The urgency: responding to God's voice *today*, while progress remains possible and opportunities exist.

## How These Two Connect

Both articles address the same target: spiritual isolation, whether experienced personally (Alone?) or inflicted communally (the Assault). Satan's strategy in both cases is the same: convince God's servants that they are cut off. The counter in both cases is the same: the reality of communion in the Holy Spirit and the recognition of spiritual warfare for what it is.
