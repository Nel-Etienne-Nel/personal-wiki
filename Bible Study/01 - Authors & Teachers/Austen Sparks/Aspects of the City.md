# Aspects of the City

[[Austin Sparks]] | Source: *Aspects of the City*

---

## The New Jerusalem as God's Standard

Drawing from 1 Kings 7 (Solomon's buildings) and Rev. 21 (the New Jerusalem), Sparks argues that God builds His dwelling place — His Church and His Bride — according to three essential characteristics: strength, beauty, and costliness. These are not aesthetic preferences; they reflect what God is and what His methods produce.

---

## Strength Through Adversity

"The Lord does not put us in glass houses to grow us. He exposes us to the bitter winds and the scorching suns of adversity and trial." God produces enduring faith not through protection but through exposure. This is not indifference to suffering; it is the recognition that strength that has never been tested is not strength at all.

Eph. 6:10 — "be strong in the Lord and in the strength of His might" — is not an exhortation to effort but to a derived, God-sourced strength that comes through adversity, not around it.

---

## Beauty Through Grace

Where suffering produces strength, grace produces beauty. The two work together: hardship without grace produces hardness; grace without hardship produces softness. Together they produce what Sparks describes as mellowness — a balance exemplified in Jesus: simultaneously immovable in conviction and gentle in manner, simultaneously strong and beautiful.

Spiritual beauty is not pleasantness or religious charm. It is the quality of a life where grace has done its work in the material that adversity tested.

---

## Costliness as True Value

God invests enormously — time, energy, material — to produce genuine spiritual value. Sparks uses Madame Curie's extraction of radium: tons of material processed to yield a few grams of the truly valuable substance. God works with the same ratio. The process looks wasteful to natural observation; the result is uniquely precious.

The implication: evaluate everything through spiritual rather than material worth. The costly thing is not the impressive thing; it is the genuinely valuable thing. Rev. 21:19 — the foundations of the City are precious stones, not common ones.
