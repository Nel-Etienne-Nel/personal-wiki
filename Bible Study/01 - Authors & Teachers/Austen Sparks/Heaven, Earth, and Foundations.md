# Heaven, Earth, and Foundations

[[Austin Sparks]] | Sources: *As It Was in the Beginning*, *"As in Heaven, So on Earth"*, *At the Crossroads*

---

## As It Was in the Beginning — Returning to Spirit-Led Foundations

Contemporary Christianity is fragmented and man-centred because it has deviated from its original character. The correction is not innovation but *return* — understanding how God operated "at the beginning" and allowing that to govern faith and practice now.

God's historical pattern: the Spirit has always been the originating and sustaining principle.
- Creation: "the Spirit of God moved upon the face of the waters" (Gen. 1:1–2)
- Exodus: the pillar of cloud/fire — Spirit-guidance made visible
- Tabernacle construction: craftsmen Spirit-filled for the work
- Jesus' ministry: born of the Spirit, baptised by the Spirit, resurrected by the Spirit
- The Church: born of the Spirit at Pentecost — Body consistent with Head

Two essential Spirit functions that defined the early church and must define the present:
1. **Power for witness** — "ye shall receive power, when the Holy Ghost is come upon you" (Acts 1:8). Peter's transformation from fearful denier to bold witness is the demonstration.
2. **Teaching discernment** — the Spirit within provides moral and doctrinal guidance without requiring external human authority exclusively.

The fragmentation of denominations — each claiming Christian authority, all contradicting each other — is itself evidence of human influence displacing Spirit-governance. Rev. 2–3: "He that hath an ear, let him hear what the Spirit saith to the churches." The Spirit is still speaking; the question is whether the churches are listening or are too invested in their own structures.

## As in Heaven, So on Earth — Prayer and the Heavenly Standard

Matt. 6:10: "Thy will be done, as in heaven, so on earth." This is not a general aspiration; it is a specific petition addressed by those born into God's family — "Our Father" presupposes family membership through spiritual rebirth (John 3:7). The prayer is corporate and bounded.

Three characteristics of how God's will is done in heaven:
1. **Single-minded devotion** to God's glory alone, never personal advantage
2. **Wholehearted commitment** — "I delight to do thy will" (Ps. 40:8), not reluctant compliance
3. **Perfect obedience** — demonstrated supremely in Christ's willingness to accept crucifixion

The mechanism of earthly transformation: the Holy Spirit reveals Christ inwardly, gradually shaping speech, actions, and decisions toward heavenly standards. This is not external imitation but inward revelation. Heaven comes down not through programme but through Person.

## At the Crossroads — Diagnosing Spiritual Drift

Jer. 6:16: "Stand ye in the ways and see, and ask for the old paths, where is the good way." Sparks diagnoses a spiritual crisis caused by a "false current" that displaced heavenly authority with earthly concerns — tangible, popular, institutional. The result: confusion, division, and weary souls.

The diagnostic sequence Sparks reads from Jeremiah:
1. **Stand** — pause and reflect at the crossroads; stop moving long enough to see
2. **Ask** — reconsider past choices; compare present conditions to the old paths
3. **Walk** — commit to repentance and decisive action through humble, courageous resolve
4. **Find** — experience the rest promised through heavenly rule

The anchor verse for the recovery: Jer. 17:12 — "A glorious throne, set on high from the beginning, is the place of our sanctuary." Heaven's governance — not improved church programmes or theological precision — provides genuine refuge and spiritual restoration.

## How These Three Connect

All three address the relationship between heavenly reality and earthly expression. "As It Was in the Beginning" identifies the Spirit as the original foundation and calls for return to Him. "As in Heaven, So on Earth" defines what that heavenly standard looks like and how it comes down through prayer and the Spirit's inward work. "At the Crossroads" diagnoses the crisis produced when that foundation is abandoned and identifies the path back.
