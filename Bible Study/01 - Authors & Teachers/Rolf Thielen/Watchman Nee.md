

**Watchman Nee** (倪柝声, 1903–1972) — one of the most significant Chinese Christian leaders of the 20th century. The phonetic approximation makes sense depending on how the name was heard or transliterated.

Here's an overview of who he was and the movement he was part of:

**Watchman Nee** was born Ni Tuosheng in Fuzhou, China, and converted to Christianity at age 17. He became a prolific teacher and church planter, and founded what became known as the **Local Church movement** (sometimes called the "Little Flock"). His core conviction was that the New Testament pattern for the church was one assembly per city — the "local church" as the expression of the Body of Christ in a given locality, rather than denominational structures.

His books became enormously influential worldwide — _The Normal Christian Life_, _Sit, Walk, Stand_, _The Spiritual Man_, and _Changed into His Likeness_ among them.

What might interest you particularly, given your engagement with T. Austin-Sparks: **Nee and Austin-Sparks had a significant relationship**. They corresponded and Nee visited Austin-Sparks in London. They shared deep convictions about Christ as the center of all things and were both critical of institutional Christianity. The influence ran in both directions.

Nee was arrested by the Communist government in 1952 and spent the last 20 years of his life in prison, dying there in 1972.

His co-worker **Witness Lee** later brought his teachings to the West, founding what became known as the **Local Churches** (or Living Stream Ministry)
