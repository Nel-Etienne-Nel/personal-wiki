# Genesis

**Overview:** The book of beginnings — creation, the fall, the flood, and the patriarchs. God creates the world and humanity, humanity rebels, and God begins his rescue plan through one family. The narrative moves from cosmic catastrophe to covenant promise.

---

### Key Moments

- **Creation (Gen 1–2):** God speaks the world into existence; humanity made in His image and given dominion
- **The Fall (Gen 3):** Adam and Eve rebel; sin and death enter creation; God pronounces the first promise of a redeemer — the seed of the woman will crush the serpent's head (3:15)
- **Cain and Abel (Gen 4):** The first murder; sin spreading outward from the garden
- **The Flood (Gen 6–9):** God judges a world consumed by violence; Noah and his family preserved; the rainbow covenant
- **Tower of Babel (Gen 11):** Humanity unified in pride; God scatters the nations and diversifies language
- **The call of Abram (Gen 12):** God calls one man out of paganism with a threefold promise — land, descendants, and blessing to all nations through him
- **The covenant with Abraham (Gen 15, 17):** God formalises the promise through a covenant; circumcision established as its sign
- **The near-sacrifice of Isaac (Gen 22):** Abraham asked to give back the very thing God gave him; God provides a substitute at the last moment
- **Jacob and Esau (Gen 25–36):** The younger displaces the older; Jacob wrestles with God at Peniel and is renamed Israel
- **Joseph (Gen 37–50):** Sold into slavery by his brothers, falsely imprisoned, rises to second-in-command in Egypt, and saves his family — and all Egypt — from famine

---

### Main Characters

**Adam, Eve** — the first humans; their choice shapes all that follows. **Cain, Abel** — the first brothers; the first murder. **Noah** — the man who walked with God in a corrupt generation. **Abraham** — the founding patriarch; called out of nothing into covenant. **Sarah** — Abraham's wife; mother of the promise despite old age. **Hagar, Ishmael** — the alternate line; not the covenant child, but not forgotten. **Isaac** — the child of promise; almost sacrificed. **Rebekah** — Isaac's wife; a woman of decisive action. **Jacob/Israel** — the man who wrestles with God and limps home changed. **Rachel, Leah** — Jacob's wives; their rivalry shapes the twelve tribes. **Joseph** — the dreamer; the one sold and exalted.

---

### Jerusalem

Not mentioned by name, but the city of **Salem** in Genesis 14 — where the mysterious priest-king **Melchizedek** blesses Abraham and receives a tithe — is the same site that becomes Jerusalem. This is one of the earliest and most loaded shadows in the entire OT. The book of Hebrews picks it back up.

---

### Connection to Jesus

Genesis 3:15 — the "seed of the woman" who crushes the serpent — is the first hint of a coming redeemer, the *protoevangelium*. The near-sacrifice of Isaac on Moriah (the same hill as Jerusalem) is a direct type: a father offering his son, a substitute provided at the last moment. Joseph's story maps structurally onto Jesus — rejected by his own, sold for silver, suffers unjustly, rises to save the very ones who betrayed him.
