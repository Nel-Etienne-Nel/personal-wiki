# Leviticus

**Overview:** The instruction manual for Israel's priests and worship. Covers the five sacrificial offerings, purity laws, the Day of Atonement, and the Holiness Code. Dense and often skipped — but nearly every major NT statement about the cross assumes this book as background.

---

### Key Moments

- **The five offerings (Lev 1–7):** Burnt, grain, peace, sin, and guilt offerings — each addressing a different dimension of Israel's relationship with God
- **Consecration of the priests (Lev 8–9):** Aaron and his sons formally set apart; the glory of God appears and fire consumes the first sacrifice
- **Nadab and Abihu (Lev 10):** Aaron's two sons offer "unauthorized fire" before the LORD and are consumed; a stark statement that approaching God is not casual business
- **The purity laws (Lev 11–15):** What is clean and unclean; covering food, skin disease, bodily discharges — practical holiness embedded in daily life
- **The Day of Atonement — Yom Kippur (Lev 16):** The one day the high priest enters the Holy of Holies; two goats — one sacrificed as a sin offering, one (the scapegoat) has Israel's sins confessed over it and is driven into the wilderness
- **The Holiness Code (Lev 17–27):** Ethics, festivals, sexual ethics, care for the poor and stranger; the repeated refrain — *"Be holy, for I the LORD your God am holy"*
- **The Year of Jubilee (Lev 25):** Every fiftieth year, debts cancelled, slaves freed, land returned to original owners

---

### Main Characters

**Moses** — receives and transmits these instructions. **Aaron** — the first high priest; the system of worship is built around his office. **Nadab and Abihu** — their death is a hinge moment in the book.

---

### Connection to Jesus

The entire sacrificial system is the conceptual grammar the NT uses to interpret the cross. The Day of Atonement (Lev 16) is the direct backdrop for the book of Hebrews — Jesus as the high priest who enters the true Holy of Holies with his own blood. The scapegoat is one of the most vivid pictures of substitution in all of Scripture. Lev 16:2 — only the high priest, once a year — makes the tearing of the Temple veil at the crucifixion (Matt 27:51) theologically explosive: the barrier is gone. Jesus reads from Isaiah 61 in Luke 4 and his *"year of the LORD's favour"* is the Jubilee of Lev 25 made flesh.
