# Exodus

**Overview:** Israel enslaved in Egypt; God raises up Moses to lead them out through ten plagues and the parting of the Red Sea. They receive the Law at Sinai and detailed instructions for the Tabernacle — the portable dwelling of God's presence among his people.

---

### Key Moments

- **Moses in the basket (Ex 2):** Hidden by his mother, found by Pharaoh's daughter, raised in the palace he will one day confront
- **The burning bush (Ex 3):** God appears in fire that doesn't consume; reveals his name — YHWH, "I AM WHO I AM" — and commissions Moses
- **The ten plagues (Ex 7–12):** Each plague targets an Egyptian deity; the escalating judgment of a God more powerful than the gods of empire
- **Passover (Ex 12):** The blood of a lamb on the doorposts; the angel of death passes over every household marked with it; Israel leaves Egypt the same night
- **Crossing the Red Sea (Ex 14):** The waters part; Israel walks through on dry ground; Pharaoh's army destroyed
- **Manna in the wilderness (Ex 16):** Bread from heaven, given daily; the test of trusting God one day at a time
- **The Ten Commandments at Sinai (Ex 20):** The moral core of the covenant; God speaks directly from the mountain
- **The golden calf (Ex 32):** Israel's first great betrayal — while Moses is on the mountain receiving God's word, they build an idol; 3,000 die
- **Moses's intercession (Ex 32–33):** One of the great intercessory prayers in Scripture; Moses pleads and God relents
- **The Tabernacle (Ex 25–40):** God gives precise blueprints for a portable dwelling; the cloud of glory fills it when complete

---

### Main Characters

**Moses** — the defining figure; reluctant prophet, mediator, lawgiver, and intercessor. **Aaron** — Moses's brother; high priest; weak under pressure. **Miriam** — Moses's sister; prophetess; leads the women in song after the sea crossing. **Pharaoh** — the unnamed king whose hardness of heart drives the plague sequence. **Jethro** — Moses's father-in-law; a Midianite priest with wisdom about delegation (Ex 18). **Joshua** — Moses's aide; first appears here.

---

### Jerusalem

No direct mention, but the Tabernacle system introduced here — God dwelling among his people in a specific, consecrated space — is the pattern the Jerusalem Temple will later fulfill in permanent form.

---

### Connection to Jesus

The Passover lamb (Ex 12) is the most direct OT type for the crucifixion. Paul is explicit: *"Christ, our Passover lamb, has been sacrificed"* (1 Cor 5:7). The manna from heaven prefigures Jesus's *"I am the bread of life"* (John 6). Moses as mediator of the old covenant foreshadows Jesus as mediator of the new. The sacrificial system introduced here is the entire framework through which the NT interprets the cross.
